Curriculum vitae

I’m currently a 5th year graduate student in the mathematical biology research program. Later this year, I’ll start a postdoc at the Courant Institute.

My interests center around using mathematics to understand how biological systems function because of randomness, rather than in spite of it. One such example (and my primary biological focus) is the utilization of molecular motors. Work I have done in this realm includes:

  1. analysis of metastable switching in populations of motors;
  2. development of theory to study state-dependent jump-diffusion processes;
  3. collaboration with experimentalists to understand motor-driven transport data.
